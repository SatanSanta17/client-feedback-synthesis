# Product Videos — Plan & Voiceover Script

Two videos derived from one recording session:

1. **Long-form demo** (~110 seconds) — full end-to-end product walkthrough, used on YouTube, in sales decks, in onboarding emails.
2. **Landing-page hero** (~30 seconds) — silent, autoplay-friendly cut lifted from the long-form. Lives above the fold or near the product showcase.

Recording strategy: shoot the long-form once, then edit the 30s cut from those takes. No new captures.

---

## Narrative arc

**Problem → Product → Outcome**, with the Product middle structured as the three-step story already on the landing page (Capture → Extract → Understand).

The headline already promises "every client conversation → product signal." The video's job is to *prove* it, not re-explain. Personas appear at the end (after the magic), not the start.

---

## Long-form video — Scene Breakdown

Total target: 110s. Shoot at 2560×1440 minimum. Hide bookmarks bar, system notifications, and any internal-only tooling. Use the demo profile with cleaned, realistic seed data.

VO style: warm, confident, declarative. Pauses between scenes are intentional — don't fill every second.

### Scene 1 — Hook: feedback is everywhere and nowhere
**Time:** 0:00 – 0:08 (8s)
**Visual:** Fast montage. Quick cuts (~1.2s each):
- Slack thread scrolling past
- Notion doc list with 30+ entries
- Zoom transcript wall of text
- Scribbled notebook page being flipped

**Voiceover:**
> "Every week, your team has dozens of client conversations. The insights are everywhere — and nowhere."

**Word count:** 16 words. **Spoken time:** ~6.2s. Breathing room: 1.8s before Scene 2.

**Production notes:**
- Each cut should feel slightly more chaotic than the last
- Background music ramps up subtly through this scene
- No on-screen text needed — VO + visuals carry it

### Scene 2 — Stakes: decisions made on memory

**Two versions to record/edit. Pick per channel.**

**Time:** 0:08 – 0:15 (7s)

#### Version A — Professional (default)
**Visual:** No human on screen. Camera/screen pans across the *artifacts* of overwhelm:
- Stack of PDF call notes growing taller
- Tabs multiplying in a browser window
- Doc folder filling up with files, timestamps ticking forward
- A clock or calendar in the corner showing time elapsing
- Final beat: a search bar with "what did Acme say about pricing?" — zero useful results

The frustration is implied through accumulation and the failed search, not through a person.

**Use for:** Landing page, sales deck, investor demos, anywhere that needs to feel polished and brand-safe.

**Veo 3 prompt:**

```
A 7-second cinematic shot in a premium SaaS commercial style, photographed
like Apple keynote B-roll. Photorealistic, no people visible. The camera
moves slowly and deliberately through a quiet workspace, revealing the
silent accumulation of abbundant client feedback.

The shot opens overhead on a wooden desk where a stack of printed PDF
meeting notes builds itself in time-lapse — sheets stacking on top of
one another, the pile rising higher than seems reasonable. The camera
tilts up smoothly to a laptop screen where browser tabs multiply across
the top bar — five, ten, twenty tabs, each titled with a client name
and meeting date. A small clock in the corner of the screen ticks
forward fast, hours blurring into days.

The camera pushes in toward a desktop folder window where files appear
one by one, timestamps stamping themselves onto each new entry —
"client-call-Acme.pdf", "Globex-discovery.txt", "Initech-QBR.docx",
"Stark-renewal-notes.md" — the folder fills until items begin to
spill out of the visible frame.

Final beat: the camera lands on a search bar at the top of the
window. A query types itself letter by letter: "what did Acme say
about pricing?" — the return key presses — and the result panel
below shows only "No results found." The cursor blinks on the empty
result. Hold for one beat, then cut to black.

Color grade: cool blue-teal cast, slightly desaturated, the visual
mood of a long quiet afternoon at a desk. Shallow depth of field on
the desk shots, sharp focus on screens. Subtle motion blur on the
time-lapse stacking. Warm desk lamp glow contrasts with cold ambient
office light from off-frame.

Audio: no dialogue, no music. Diegetic ambient only — soft paper
rustling as the stack grows, faint keyboard clicks, the muted click
of new tabs opening, quiet keystrokes as the search query is typed,
and a single soft chime when "No results found" appears. A voiceover
will be added in post.

Pacing: deliberate, building. Not panicked — the slow suffocation
of accumulating unanswered questions. Each beat slightly heavier
than the last.

16:9, 4K, 60fps.
```

### Scene 3 — Reveal: the product
**Time:** 0:15 – 0:22 (7s)
**Visual:** Hard cut from chaos to a clean, calm Synthesiser dashboard hero shot. Fade in the wordmark. One beat of stillness — the visual contrast does the heavy lifting.

**Voiceover:**
> "Synthesiser turns every client conversation into a product signal."

**Word count:** 9 words. **Spoken time:** ~3.5s.

**Production notes:**
- Match the headline copy exactly so anyone arriving from the landing page recognises the line
- Subtle whoosh / reset sound effect on the cut
- Music shifts from "tense buildup" to "confident product theme"

### Scene 4 — Capture
**Time:** 0:22 – 0:38 (16s)
**Visual:** Screen recording of the capture flow.
- Open with cursor pasting raw, messy call notes into the textarea (~3s)
- Quick cut to file upload — drag-drop a Slack export `.txt`, then a PDF, then a CSV (~5s, sped up 1.5×)
- Cut back to the notes textarea, full of content, ready to structure (~3s)

**Voiceover:**
> "Paste your notes after a call. Drop in a Slack export, a WhatsApp thread, a PDF, a CSV. If your team talked about it, Synthesiser captures it."

**Word count:** 28 words. **Spoken time:** ~10.7s.

**Production notes:**
- Record this scene 2–3 times — pick the cleanest cursor movement
- Speed-ramp file uploads to 1.3–1.5× (no one wants to watch you drag a file)
- Real-looking client name in the notes ("Acme Logistics — Q2 review call")

---

### Scene 5 — Extract (the magic moment)
**Time:** 0:38 – 0:55 (17s)
**Visual:** This is the aha. Don't rush.
- Cursor hits the Structure button (~1s)
- Loading state, then signals materialise: themes appear with tags, sentiment chips fade in, action items list builds row by row (~8s)
- Camera/zoom pushes in slightly as the structured output fills the panel
- End on the fully-populated structured view — clean, organised, tagged

**Voiceover:**
> "Hit Structure. In seconds, AI extracts the themes, the sentiment, the action items. Every signal tagged, every quote linked back to where it came from."

**Word count:** 26 words. **Spoken time:** ~10s.

**Production notes:**
- This scene is the whole video. Worth recording 4–5 takes
- If the live AI call is too slow or variable, fake the timing in post — viewers expect "seconds," not the literal API duration
- Music swells subtly as signals appear; ease back as the panel settles

---

### Scene 6 — Understand, Part 1: the dashboard
**Time:** 0:55 – 1:15 (20s)
**Visual:** Cut to the insights dashboard.
- Wide shot of the full dashboard (~3s)
- Pan/zoom to the sentiment trend line — animate the line drawing in if possible (~5s)
- Cut to theme matrix, hover one cell, tooltip appears (~5s)
- Cut to "trending up / trending down" callout (~5s)
- End with a wide shot again — showing the whole landscape

**Voiceover:**
> "Your dashboard shows the entire client landscape. Sentiment shifts. Trending themes. The signals heating up across every account, every conversation, every team."

**Word count:** 23 words. **Spoken time:** ~8.8s. Lots of breathing room — the visuals carry it.

**Production notes:**
- Demo data must look real here. A theme matrix with one row of fake data ruins the scene
- Use cursor highlights / soft callout pulses on the elements being mentioned
- Don't move the cursor while VO is silent — let the data speak

---

### Scene 7 — Understand, Part 2: ask anything (RAG chat)
**Time:** 1:15 – 1:38 (23s)
**Visual:** Cut to the chat interface.
- Empty state, cursor in the input (~2s)
- Type the question (sped up): "What are the top concerns from enterprise clients in the last 30 days?" (~4s)
- Hit send. Answer streams in, word by word (~10s)
- Citation chips appear under the answer — hover one, mini-popover showing the source session (~5s)
- End on the answer + citations visible together (~2s)

**Voiceover:**
> "Need an answer? Ask in plain English. Synthesiser searches every session your team has ever captured, and answers — with citations to the exact call, the exact quote. No more scrolling through call notes. No more guessing what clients want."

**Word count:** 43 words. **Spoken time:** ~16.5s.

**Production notes:**
- The streaming answer is a hero moment — don't speed-ramp it, let it feel real
- Citation hover popover must show real, recognisable session names
- If chat latency is high, cut the dead time in editing — viewers shouldn't see a long blank wait

### Scene 8 — Personas
**Time:** 1:38 – 1:50 (12s)
**Visual:** Four-up grid or quick-cut sequence. ~3s per persona.
- Founder reviewing dashboard before a board meeting
- Sales rep checking a prospect's competitor mentions
- CS manager scanning churn signals
- PM filtering themes for the roadmap

Each persona = one product view they'd actually use. Match the persona to the product surface that serves them.

**Voiceover:**
> "Founders. Sales. Customer Success. Product. Everyone running on the same evidence."

**Word count:** 11 words. **Spoken time:** ~4.2s. Words land on each persona cut.

**Production notes:**
- Keep this short — overstaying breaks the momentum into the CTA
- Captions in the corner of each cut: "Founder," "Sales," etc.

**Visual:** Synthesiser wordmark center-screen. URL fades in below. Soft brand glow.

**Voiceover:**

**Word count:** 11 words. **Spoken time:** ~4.2s.

## Pre-production checklist

Before recording, confirm:

- [ ] Demo profile has realistic seed data: 8–12 sessions across 4–5 clients, varied sentiment, a populated theme matrix, at least 2–3 chat-worthy questions
- [ ] Browser profile is clean: no bookmarks bar, no extension icons, no notification badges
- [ ] System notifications muted (Do Not Disturb on)
- [ ] Mouse cursor is visible and at default speed
- [ ] Screen recording at 2560×1440 minimum, 60fps preferred
- [ ] Microphone tested if recording VO live (otherwise record VO separately in a quiet room)
- [ ] Music track selected and licensed (or royalty-free)
- [ ] Meme clip received from Burhan for Scene 2 Version B
